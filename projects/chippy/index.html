3lb Combat Robot | April 2024 - Current

Chippy

Stats

  • Weight: 3 lbs (Beetleweight class)
  • Weapon: 4.5″ AR600 steel blade, 400 MPH
  • Materials: Steel, TPU, UHMW, Carbon Fiber, Aluminum
  • Record: 8-2

Overview

Chippy is a 3 lb hammersaw–style BattleBot built to strike and pierce opposing robots using a spinning saw blade mounted on an articulating arm. Designed and first built in January 2024, Chippy emphasizes energy absorption, aggressive undercutting with steel forks, and modular iteration. It has competed in the NHRL (National Havoc Robot League) twice.

Weapon System

Chippy’s weapon is a 4.5″ AR600 blade driven by a through-bolted RDY-MINI-5022-V2 hub motor that spins at 400 mph; the hub mounts to a UHMW arm with a 3:1 gear reduction that converts the motor’s rotation into a swinging strike.

Armor

A defining feature of Chippy is its configurable armor system: the outer shell and front forks can be swapped or reshaped depending on the opponent. That lets me tune fork geometry and material, or change armor thickness and layout, to give Chippy an advantage.

Versions & Iterations

V1 — Apr 2024

Weapon arm lacked torque and couldn’t reliably flip or recover.

V2 — Oct 2024

Redesigned forks and increased arm gear ratio for consistent strikes.

V3 — Apr 2025

Refined geometry and layout for durability and faster repairs.

Future Plans

Chippy is currently going through a 3.1 design iteration and will hopefully compete next in early 2026.
